## Design Context

### Users
Two equally important audiences:
- **Ticket buyers** — regular people attending events, many with no crypto knowledge. They interact with the shop, ticket wallet, and QR code at the door. Need simplicity, reassurance, and zero jargon.
- **Event organisers** — create events, track sales, manage the dashboard. More comfortable with technology but not necessarily crypto-native. Expect clarity and efficiency.
- Doormen are a third, secondary role — they use the scanner briefly at the gate.

### Brand Personality
**Premium and trustworthy** — people are spending real money. The platform must feel as solid as a real ticketing company, not a Web3 experiment. Three words: **confident, clean, alive.**
Emotional goal: users should feel like they're holding something real and trustworthy, not a lottery ticket or a JPEG.
Primary gap to close: the UI is functional but forgettable — nothing distinctly says "this is Passly."

### Aesthetic Direction — "Tokn Based" template (2026-07-06)
- The binding reference is the Claude-Design prototype `Tokn Based.html` in the repo root — the site should look exactly like it (branded Passly).
- **Light theme**: white surfaces on `oklch(0.987 0.004 280)`, dark ink text. Violet accent driven by `--hue: 285` (`--accent: oklch(0.54 0.22 var(--hue))`). Soft radii (6/10/14px), subtle layered shadows, washes (`--accent-wash`, `--ok-wash`, …) for status surfaces.
- **Typography**: Geist (UI) + Geist Mono (serials, links), loaded in the root layout.
- **Tokens and component CSS** (`.btn`, `.card`, `.chip`, `.drawer`, …) live in `src/app/globals.css`; `Icon`/`Spark` components in `src/app/components/passlyUi.tsx`. Pages add page-specific styles via inline `<style>` blocks.
- **Language**: German copy everywhere.
- Migration status: **complete** (2026-07-06) — every page uses the light template. The dark "Marquee" palette and Unbounded/Epilogue fonts are gone.
- Anti-references: crypto/web3 wording (banned), dark neon crypto aesthetics, Ticketmaster clutter.

### Design Principles
1. **Invisible complexity** — on-chain mechanics never surface in the UI. No wallet addresses in primary copy, no "mint" or "cNFT" jargon for buyers.
2. **Restraint with the accent** — the violet accent appears only on primary actions, status indicators, and brand moments. Overuse kills its power.
3. **Consistent spatial rhythm** — pages share the same topbar pattern, card language, and spacing cadence. Cohesion across buyer and organiser flows matters.
4. **Friendly confidence** — copy is short, direct, warm. Interfaces don't over-explain or hedge.
5. **Mobile-first interactions** — buyers use phones at concerts; doormen scan on phones. Desktop is secondary for buyers, primary for organisers creating events.
